05 — TikTok UGC

Three Specific "Moments to Capture"

1
"The Tap Reveal" — Fan UGC
Influencer

Fan holds card for first time, taps phone, watches streaming link open. Captures surprise + excitement + showing a friend.

Specs: Rear camera, portrait, 8–10 sec min, dim lighting OK, no text overlays.

Caption: "POV: Your tap takes them straight to the album"

2
"The Merch Table Walk-Through" — Band Account
Influencer

Band member walks phone across merch table, stops on Custom Goodz, explains what it is. Ends with fan picking one up.

Specs: 30–45 sec, natural lighting, show other merch alongside, close-up of card with artwork visible.

Caption: "Our merch table has something new this tour 🎵"

3
"The Backstage Design Story" — Album Release Cycle
Influencer

Band member shows artwork on laptop, then physical card reveal, then tap demo. 60–90 sec.

Specs: Ring light OK, include trending audio for first 3 sec as hook.

Caption: "We put our whole album on one card — here's the story behind it"

Posting cadence: Week 1–2: 5 posts/week. Week 3–4: 3–5 posts/week. Any video hitting 10K organic views → run $50 Spark Ads immediately. Don't wait.
06 — The Bold Idea

Dreamed Up at Midnight. Not in the Original Plan.

The "Tap to Hear" Campaign
"Name a band whose album reveal should come with one of these 👇"

Mechanic: On release day, band films TikTok revealing the album through the card. Video shows card being tapped, album page opening. Caption invites fans to tag their favorite bands.

Why it goes viral: Rewards the in-person audience ("I was there"). Creates collection mechanics. Fans tag bands → bands get tagged → bands contact Goodz organically. The growth loop runs on fan advocacy, not ads.

Dollar figures: 50-card run = ~$170 cost. Band gets $600–750 revenue. Goodz gets: earned media, UGC library, referral pipeline — all without spending a dollar on ads.

07 — Merch Table Objection

The 15-Word Explainer Script

The hidden objection

"I don't want to be the band that made their fans tap a thing."

At 11pm after a show, with 8 people in line, the explainer has to compete with the t-shirt transaction in 30 seconds. The fix isn't more detail — it's less.

"These link directly to our album — tap with your phone and it opens right up."
15 words. Include in every band partner kit.
08 — Audience Expansion

Too Indie-Rock Centric. Two Huge Missed Segments.

Beat Producers
Insider

Already selling beat cards and sample packs. Natural extension: physical beat cards with NFC links to download pages. Already understand the value proposition. Zero education required.

Where to find them: Beat Discord servers, beat-making Substack newsletters, BeatStars community.

Bedroom Pop Artists
Insider

Lowest barrier to entry. Primarily digital, strong Bandcamp integration, super-fans want something physical. 50-unit minimum is a real test without being a massive commitment.

Where to find them: Bandcamp's "Bedroom Pop" editorial list, TikTok's "Bedroom Pop" hashtag community.
