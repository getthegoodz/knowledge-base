# Goodz B2C Mixtape Gift landing wireframe

Date: 2026-04-02

## Purpose

Translate the B2C landing/checkout brief into a concrete page structure for a cold-traffic Mixtape Goodz landing page.

This wireframe is the structure behind the first HTML mockup in:
- `goodz/prototypes/b2c-mixtape-landing-v1/index.html`

## Page type

- dedicated B2C Mixtape Gift landing page
- designed for cold paid-social traffic
- optimized for gift buyers first
- intended to hand off into Shopify or a purchase/customization flow

## Section outline

### 1. Hero
Goal:
- explain the product in seconds
- frame it as a meaningful physical mixtape gift
- create immediate purchase curiosity

Current direction:
- headline: `Build them a mixtape they can hold`
- subhead focuses on a personalized set of music cards for the streaming era
- primary CTA: `Create your mixtape`
- secondary CTA: `See how it works`
- confidence bar: no app required / works with Spotify + Apple Music / ships in a few days
- hero visual should combine:
  - crate of Goodz
  - product cards
  - phone preview of recipient experience

### 2. How it works
Goal:
- make the product feel easy and intuitive

Three-step structure:
1. choose your set
2. pick the music
3. give a gift they can tap and play

### 3. Why it feels special
Goal:
- elevate the product beyond utility

Current messaging buckets:
- more personal than sending a link
- built for streaming-era music lovers
- a gift that remains visible in the real world

### 4. Personalization / Spotify import teaser
Goal:
- make the purchase flow feel more magical before checkout

Current direction:
- show how each Good could be assigned to a playlist/theme
- tease optional Spotify import
- communicate that importing is helpful but not required

### 5. Gift scenarios / examples
Goal:
- help cold traffic immediately map the product to a use case

Current buckets:
- for your partner
- for your best friend
- for the music person in your life

### 6. FAQ / confidence builder
Goal:
- reduce friction and answer known concerns without making the page feel technical

Current questions:
- do I need an app?
- do I need Spotify?
- do they come preloaded?
- how fast do they ship?

### 7. Final CTA block
Goal:
- reassert the emotional promise and push into action

Current direction:
- repeat the core message
- make the final CTA obvious

## Conversion thesis behind the page

The page is trying to shift the user story from:
- `what is this weird product?`

to:
- `oh, this is a beautiful personalized music gift — I want to make one`

## Known next iterations

1. improve visual hierarchy and polish
2. decide whether the CTA should open:
   - Shopify PDP
   - a lightweight pre-checkout builder
   - or a richer external configurator
3. test alternate hero tone variants:
   - broader meaningful gift
   - more romantic
   - more nostalgic / `the mixtape is back`
4. decide whether to add social proof / testimonials / creator content above the fold or below it
