Goodz overnight research — checkout + growth ideas

Prepared by Janet · March 30, 2026

Bottom line: the opportunity is probably not “make Shopify checkout cooler.” It’s to move value creation upstream of checkout by helping people create a gift, fan object, event keepsake, or branded experience before payment.

What I could verify tonight

I reviewed the main marketing site plus supporting pages on getthegoodz.com, and limited content from the Shopify storefront on shop.getthegoodz.com.

  • NFC-enabled physical collectible
  • Made of basswood with printed artwork
  • Links to music, playlists, videos, photos, event pages, and more
  • Used for both direct-to-consumer collectibles and custom projects for artists, weddings, events, and businesses

Core business read

Goodz is not really selling a wooden NFC object. Depending on the buyer, it’s selling:

  1. A music-object gift
  2. A fandom collectible
  3. A memory-delivery object

Highest-upside product directions

1) Consumer “Make a Mixtape” flow

The strongest immediate concept. Turn purchase into a gift creation experience, not just a SKU checkout.

  • Choose occasion
  • Select playlist / album / landing-page content
  • Add title, note, photo, or art
  • Preview physical + digital experience
  • Add gift extras and checkout

2) Spotify OAuth as enhancement, not requirement

Good use: import playlists, saved albums, and recent listening. Bad use: making Spotify login required just to purchase.

Best pattern: paste-a-link fast path + optional Connect Spotify enhanced path.

3) Event / wedding configurator

Strong fit because the site already hints at weddings and events. The better flow is structured, self-serve exploration before any contact/sales handoff.

  • Choose event type
  • Enter quantity range
  • Pick use case
  • Upload art / branding
  • See estimate range + timing

4) B2B bulk flow

B2B buyers are buying a campaign / activation, not a product page SKU. The flow should feel like ordering a branded experience.

Best target segments include musicians/labels, DJs, wedding planners, event agencies, hospitality, conferences, brands, museums, nonprofits, and corporate gifting.

5) Artist/fan checkout should lean harder into fandom

Emphasize scarcity, unlocks, bundles, numbered editions, and collector logic. Fans buy meaning and exclusivity, not just utility.

Most important prototype recommendation

Goodz Studio: Make a Mixtape Gift

This is the highest-confidence first prototype.

  • Choose template
  • Paste Spotify / Apple Music URL
  • Optional Spotify connect
  • Add title, message, and photo
  • Preview landing page
  • Send configured product into checkout

Recommended roadmap

Phase 1

  1. Occasion-based entry points
  2. Simple pre-checkout mixtape/customization flow
  3. Structured custom-order flow with estimate range
  4. Dedicated pages for weddings, artists, business events, hospitality

Phase 2

  1. Optional Spotify OAuth
  2. Richer landing-page templates
  3. Sample-pack flow for B2B/custom

Phase 3

  1. Repeat-buyer dashboard
  2. Proofs / reorders / campaign analytics
  3. Collector and bundle mechanics

Questions to resolve next

  1. What are current top-selling products or customer segments?
  2. How custom is the current landing-page/content stack?
  3. Is Shopify long-term storefront infrastructure or just current plumbing?
  4. Is custom/B2B side business or main margin engine?
  5. Does Goodz already have proprietary tap analytics infrastructure?
