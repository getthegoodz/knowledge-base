# **GOODZ – Overview** 

## **How We Arrived at GOODZ**

1. Noticed a Shift Toward Physical-Digital Music Culture  
* Fans increasingly buying physical formats (vinyl, cassette, CD) for emotional or collectible value, even without players.  
* Physical-digital hybrids on the rise: Amiibo, Bandcamp download cards, Spotify code mixtapes, Japanese micro-records.  
* Fan-created collectibles are/were booming: Taylor Swift bracelets, K-pop light sticks, mixtape culture, NFTs.  
* No changes/innovations to music merch in years. 

2. Saw NFC-Driven Tech Opening New Doors  
* Early tools like Yoto and Toonies were promising but hardware-dependent.  
* Wearable and scannable NFC tech (e.g., IYK) hinted at lightweight, direct-to-mobile engagement.  
* Amiibo’s were already being used in gaming: why not something for music? 

**The GOODZ card format**

1. Validated Through Early User Testing/User feedback  
* User testing at Giant Robot, plus feedback via Instagram and events.  
* Strong support for tactile and personalized music sharing.  
* Users appreciated: No player dependency, simple UX, and physical keepsakes for digital content.


2. How we arrived at the Card Format:   
* No player needed   
* Existing manufacturing path (key card manufacturers)   
* Made from sustainable materials   
* Doesn’t take up much space  
* Crates could be manufactured at home, or outsourced  
* UV printed afforded high quality, collectible worthy printing with the bonus of being eco-friendly

## **What GOODZ Is Today: 3 Active Product Lines**

**1\. GOODZ Mixtape**

* NFC cards that link to custom playlists (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.).  
* Designed for gifting, sharing, and self-expression.  
* Features: No content licensing required, custom titles, stickers, graphics, and messages.


**2\. Custom GOODZ**

* Tailored for indie artists, DJs, events, weddings.  
* Low-cost, high-impact cards that link to custom landing pages (audio, video, merch, tickets).  
* Real-time content updates are possible. 


**3\. Artist GOODZ**

* Premium NFC cards for well-known artists and labels.  
* Serialized editions, exclusive designs, and custom crates.  
* Examples: TMBG (full crate experience), OutKast and Britney (design-driven drops), Jazz Crate (licensed content test).  
*  Use cases: drops, tour bundles, fan club perks.

## **Goodz – Overview**

**Goodz** is a physical-digital collectibles company co-founded in late 2023 by **Mike Rosenthal** and **Chris Cerrito**, during a period when Fanaply (Mike’s other company) was functionally out of cash. Goodz was conceived as a modern, sustainable, and tech-enabled alternative to outdated physical media (like vinyl, CDs, etc.), bridging the gap between physical ownership and digital content.

### **Core Product**

Goodz are **wooden collectibles with embedded NFC chips**. When tapped with a smartphone, they unlock exclusive, dynamic digital content (e.g., streaming albums, artist microsites, group chats, private media).

There are multiple flavors of Goodz:

* **Mixtape Goodz** – customizable, wooden “mixtapes” that link to playlists or personal audio.

* **Album Goodz / Crates** – curated releases of official albums, often in boxed sets.

* **Authenticator Goodz** – premium NTAG424-chip enabled items that gate access to locked content or fan communities.

* **Custom Goodz** – one-off branded collectibles for weddings, conferences, corporate events, private parties, etc.

Goodz are intentionally designed to be:

* **Collectible**: tactile, shareable, and unique

* **Eco-friendly**: made from basswood, avoiding plastic and vinyl

* **Gateways**: to exclusive or fan-first content, not just merch

* **Flexible**: usable by musicians, creators, brands, and even personal gifting

## **Timeline & Milestones**

### **2023**

* **Mid-2023**: Fanaply hits financial trouble. Elysian Park Ventures (Fanaply’s lead investor) temporarily halts funding.

* **Summer–Fall 2023**: Mike and Chris begin prototyping Goodz independently while still involved with Fanaply.

* **Late 2023**: Goodz soft-launches online, selling Mixtape Goodz and prototyping album drops. No investment raised—bootstrap model.

### **2024**

* **Early 2024**:

  * Goodz ships multiple B2B orders (They Might Be Giants Crates, indie labels, etc.).

  * Starts selling on D2C store with \~25% repeat purchase rate and $37 AOV.

* **Spring 2024**:

  * Launches **Original Jazz Classics Crate, Vol 1** — a deluxe 5-album set licensed from Concord Music.

  * Works with Sony to release a **Britney Spears 25th Anniversary Goodz** (Oops\!...I Did It Again).

  * Begins expanding into **custom projects**: weddings, Motorola conference, charity events.

* **Mid-2024**:

  * Total B2B Goodz shipped crosses 13,000+ units.

  * Launches Shopify-integrated SaaS content gating platform.

  * Begins exploring “Unlocker” as a standardized product for creators.

* **Fall 2024**:

  * Internal pivot begins toward **SaaS-enabled, self-serve tools** for creators and partners to manage content \+ analytics. Not yet built. 

  **2025 \- Goodz is largely dormant.**

## **Key Differentiators**

* **Technology**: Some Goodz use a patent-pending process using NTAG424 chips to verify physical tap before unlocking gated content. Some are just regular NFC chips. 

* **Sustainability**: Appeals to millennial and Gen Z buyers who prioritize eco-friendly merch.

* **Fan Utility**: Emphasis on collectible experiences vs disposable merch. Positioned as **"keys" to premium digital content.**

* **First-mover advantage** in linking tactile merch to dynamic online content via authenticated tap-to-unlock experiences.

---

## **Business Model**

* **B2B**:

  * Wholesale model for record labels, artists, conferences, events.

  * Clients presell Goodz via their own stores (e.g., Sony, Concord, Fania) and order units from Goodz wholesale.

  * Strong appeal as a new merch category and revenue stream.

* **D2C**:

  * Shopify store with core product lines (Mixtapes, Album Crates, etc.).

  * Heavy seasonal spikes (especially Q4 holidays).

  * Retargeted social campaigns focused on fans and gifting moments.

* **SaaS / Platform** (2024–2025):

  * In development: content management system \+ microsite builder for Goodz owners.

  * Analytics dashboard to show tap data, fan engagement, etc.

  * Future roadmap includes creator-facing subscription tiers.
