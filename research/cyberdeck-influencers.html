The Trend

What Is a Cyberdeck?

A cyberdeck is a DIY handheld computer — typically built around a Raspberry Pi — housed in a custom enclosure often made from thrifted cases, 3D-printed parts, or repurposed objects (Polly Pocket cases, old purses, wooden boxes).

The term is attributed to a science fiction novel from the 1980s — William Gibson's Neuromancer is often cited as the inspiration. The aesthetic is cyberpunk: CRT screens, backlit keyboards, exposed circuit boards.

"More people on TikTok are creating thrifted DIY computers called cyberdecks... Creators say the project is a way to rethink our relationship with technology, designing something that's uniquely yours."

— CNN Shorts, April 2026 (14K views)

Core appeal: Independence from Big Tech. Physical craft. Personal expression. Understanding how technology actually works.

Strategic Concept

The Boombox Cyberdeck Idea

The Concept: A Vintage Boombox That Plays Albums

Build a cyberdeck styled as a vintage boombox (or similar retro audio form factor) that has an NFC reader built in. Place a Custom Goodz card on the slot or pad — the card triggers playback of the album loaded onto it. The Goodz card is the physical interface to the album.

A carrying case/jacket holds multiple Custom Goodz cards — each one a different album.

STEP 1

NFC reader built into boombox body

STEP 2

Slot/pad where you place a Goodz card

STEP 3

Card triggers album playback on connected speakers

STEP 4

Case holds multiple cards — each a different album

The physical Spotify remote made of cardboard and nostalgia.
